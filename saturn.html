Planet Profile

Saturn — Lord of the Rings

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest in our Solar System. Its stunning ring system — made of billions of ice and rock particles — makes it one of the most recognisable objects in space.

Saturn is a gas giant, meaning it has no solid surface. If you could drop a spacecraft in, it would simply sink deeper into the gas until crushed by pressure.

Despite its enormous size, Saturn is the least dense planet in the Solar System — it is so light relative to its volume that it would float on water.

  • Distance from Sun 1.43 billion km
  • Day length 10.7 hours
  • Year length 29.4 Earth years
  • Temperature −178°C
  • Diameter 116,460 km
  • Number of moons 146 (and counting)
  • Ring groups 7 main rings

Saturn has 146 confirmed moons — more than any other planet.

  • Titan — larger than Mercury, with a thick atmosphere and liquid methane lakes
  • Enceladus — jets of water ice shoot from its south pole, hinting at a hidden ocean
  • Mimas — its huge crater makes it look remarkably like the Death Star
The Ring System

A Ring System Like No Other

Made of Ice & Rock

Saturn’s rings are made of countless particles — from tiny grains to chunks as large as a house — composed mostly of water ice with some rocky debris.

Incredibly Wide

The rings stretch up to 282,000 km from Saturn’s centre — nearly the full distance from the Earth to the Moon.

Surprisingly Thin

Despite being so wide, the rings are on average only 10–100 metres thick — thinner than a six-storey building, relative to their width.

Saturn Up Close

The Cassini Mission

NASA’s Cassini spacecraft spent 13 years orbiting Saturn, sending back stunning images and discoveries about the planet, its rings, and its moons.

Cassini revealed that Enceladus has a liquid water ocean beneath its icy surface — making it one of the best candidates in the Solar System for finding life.

Mercury
Rocky Planet

Mercury is the smallest planet and the closest to the Sun. A single day on Mercury is longer than its year — it spins very slowly but orbits the Sun incredibly fast.

77M km from Sun Day: 59 Earth days Moons: 0
Venus
Terrestrial Planet

Venus is the hottest planet at 465°C — even hotter than Mercury. A thick blanket of carbon dioxide traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect. It also spins backwards.

Temp: 465°C Day: 243 Earth days Moons: 0
Earth
Terrestrial Planet

Our home — the only planet known to support life. Earth has liquid water, a protective magnetic field, and just the right atmosphere to keep conditions stable for living things.

150M km from Sun Day: 24 hours Moons: 1
Mars
Rocky Planet

Mars has the tallest volcano in the Solar System (Olympus Mons) and a canyon that would stretch across the USA. NASA rovers are actively exploring its surface today.

Temp: −60°C avg Day: 24.6 hours Moons: 2
Jupiter
Gas Giant

Jupiter is so large that every other planet could fit inside it. Its Great Red Spot is a storm that has been swirling for over 350 years. It has 95 known moons.

Moons: 95 Day: 10 hours Mass: 318x Earth
Saturn
Gas Giant

Famous for its spectacular ring system made of ice and rock. Saturn is the least dense planet — it would float on water! It holds the record with 146 confirmed moons. Visit the full Saturn page →

7 main ring groups Moons: 146 Day: 10.7 hours
Uranus
Ice Giant

Uranus rotates on its side — its axis is tilted at 98 degrees, so it essentially rolls around the Sun. Its blue-green colour comes from methane in its atmosphere.

Temp: −224°C Moons: 28 Tilt: 98 degrees
Neptune
Ice Giant

Neptune has the strongest winds in the Solar System — up to 2,100 km/h. It was the first planet discovered through mathematical prediction rather than direct observation.

Wind: 2,100 km/h Moons: 16 Year: 165 Earth years

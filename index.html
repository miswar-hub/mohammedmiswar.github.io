Our Galaxy

The Milky Way

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy — our cosmic home. It stretches roughly 100,000 light-years across and contains between 100–400 billion stars.

Our Solar System sits about 26,000 light-years from the galactic centre, tucked inside the Orion Arm — one of the galaxy’s minor spiral arms.

At the very heart of the Milky Way lurks a supermassive black hole called Sagittarius A*, with a mass about 4 million times that of our Sun.
